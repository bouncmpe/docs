# Procedure for Cheating Behavior

## Definitions

Cheating includes, but is not limited to, the following actions:

01. Copying using unauthorized materials, copying from someone else’s paper, allowing others to copy from his/her own paper, or giving unauthorized assistance to others during a test or examination.
02. Working with others in completing a take-home examination or assignment unless the instructor has allowed independent action or allowed any specific aid could be accepted by students.
03. Submitting of material in whole or part for academic evaluation that has been prepared by another student(s), individual(s) or community agency.
04. Submitting, without prior permission of the instructor, any work by a student which has at any time been submitted in identical or similar form by that student in fulfillment of any other academic requirement at any institution.
05. Marking or submitting an examination or evaluation material in a manner designed to deceive the grading system.
06. Obtaining in a fraudulent manner any material relating to a student’s academic work (theft of examination, collusion with a university employee, etc.).
07. Attempting to influence or change an academic evaluation, grade, or record by unfair means.
08. Permitting another student to substitute for one’s self in any academic evaluation.
09. Wilfully damaging the academic work or efforts of another student.
10. Trying to deceive the instructor and/or assistant by any means such as denying the submission of a homework/project.

## Actions Against

The following actions will be taken against all sides involved in cheating:

01. The student will receive a zero or negative grade for the related test or evaluation. In addition, the student may receive the grade `F`, or one letter less grade than the normal grade depending on the severity of cheating as judged by the evaluator, for the related course. For example, an organized cheating action in a final exam may result in `F` grade.
02. The responsible instructor will file for the disciplinary action. Students must already be aware of related clauses of _YÖK Disiplin Yönetmeliği_.
03. The student will not be issued any letter of reference by any instructor of the department.
04. The student may not be accepted to any further graduate program such as the MS and PhD programs in the department.
05. The student’s name, the course, the year and the semester and the specificcheating behaviour will be logged in a departmental database for further reference to the student.
06. Regarding students of other departments, the respective department will also be informed of the specific cheating behavior.
